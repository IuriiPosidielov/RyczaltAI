VAT Registration and Taxation of Gas, Electricity, and Emissions Allowance Transfer Services (2023–2026) — Polish Tax Law

In the period from April 1, 2023, to December 31, 2026, VAT taxpayers for deliveries of gas via the gas system, electricity via the electricity system, and services related to the transfer of greenhouse gas emission allowances (as defined in the Act of June 12, 2015, on the Emissions Trading System — Journal of Laws 2024 No. 1505 and 2025 No. 303) include legal entities, organizational units without legal personality, and individuals who are buyers of gas, electricity, or recipients of such emission allowances — provided that the following conditions are jointly satisfied:

1. For gas or electricity deliveries: The buyer must be a VAT-registered active taxpayer under Article 96(4), whose primary business activity in relation to purchasing gas or electricity is resale, and whose own consumption of these goods is insignificant.
2. For emission allowance transfer services: The recipient must be a VAT-registered active taxpayer under Article 96(4).
3. Deliveries or services must be conducted within transactions directly or through an authorized entity on:
   a) a commodity exchange as defined under commodity exchange regulations;
   b) a regulated market or OTF (Organized Trading Facility) as defined under the Act of July 29, 2005, on Financial Instruments Trading.
4. The supplier or service provider must be a VAT-registered active taxpayer under Article 96(4), whose sale is not exempt from VAT under Articles 113(1) and (9) or Article 113a(1).

Additionally, the following entities are specifically considered buyers or recipients under paragraph 1, points 1 and 2:
- Commodity exchanges operating under commodity exchange regulations;
- Commodity brokerage firms or financial brokerage firms under commodity exchange regulations;
- Commodity clearing houses or clearing and settlement institutions under commodity exchange regulations, including the National Securities Depository S.A. or entities to which the National Securities Depository has delegated functions under Article 48(2) of the Act of July 29, 2005, on Financial Instruments Trading;
- Entities simultaneously operating clearing and settlement functions under the Act of July 29, 2005, on Financial Instruments Trading;
- Entities holding a concession issued by the Energy Regulatory Office under the Energy Law of April 10, 1997, enabling trading in the commodities specified in paragraph 1, or entities holding an account in the EU Register as defined in Article 8(5) of the Emissions Trading Act of June 12, 2015.
