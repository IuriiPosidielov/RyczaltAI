**Tax Law – Binding Information on VAT and Excise Duties – Article 42c**

This article establishes the scope and application of Binding Information on VAT (WIS) and Binding Information on Excise Duties (WIA) for tax authorities and taxpayers, including rules for applying provisions of Articles 14k–14m of the Tax Ordinance when WIS is amended or revoked, and when WIA classification is used beyond its validity period.

- **Paragraph 1**: WIS binds tax authorities and relevant taxpayers regarding goods delivered, imported, or intra-community acquired during its validity, services performed during its validity, and combined transactions constituting a single taxable activity during its validity.

- **Paragraphs 2–2d**: When WIS is amended or revoked, and the taxpayer continues to rely on the prior version, Articles 14k–14m of the Tax Ordinance apply accordingly, depending on the grounds for amendment or revocation and the timing of reliance (e.g., before or after notification of change or revocation).

- **Paragraph 3**: For transactions under Article 42a performed by third parties, or when a taxpayer relies on the classification of goods under a WIA beyond its validity period, Articles 14k–14m of the Tax Ordinance apply accordingly, depending on the timing of) the WIS/WIA’s publication or expiration.

**Retrieval Summary**: Tax law provisions governing the binding effect of VAT and excise duty information (WIS/WIA), including application rules during amendments, revocations, and classification reliance beyond validity, under Articles 42c and 14k–14m of the Tax Ordinance.
