VAT Law – Article 13j: Conditions for Excluding Intra-Community Supply During Substitution of VAT Taxpayer

This provision establishes the conditions under which an intra-Community supply of goods, as defined in Article 13(3), is not considered to have occurred during the period when one VAT taxpayer is substituted by another, provided the following criteria are met: (1) the substituting taxpayer must be authorized to manage the goods as owner under an agreement with the original taxpayer referenced in Article 13h(2)(2); (2) the original taxpayer must not have a business establishment or fixed place of business in the Member State to which the goods are being moved; (3) the substituting taxpayer must be identified for intra-Community transactions in another Member State, with their name, identity number, and identification number assigned by that Member State known to the original taxpayer at the time of substitution; (4) the original taxpayer must report the substituting taxpayer’s identification number in the summary declaration under Article 100(1)(5); and (5) the substitution must be recorded in the taxpayer’s register under Article 109(11c).
