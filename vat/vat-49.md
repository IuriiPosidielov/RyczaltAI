**Tax Exemption for Imported Goods from Inheritances – Polish Tax Law**

1. Tax exemption applies to imported goods received by:
   - A natural person residing within the country’s territory, or
   - A non-profit legal entity operating a non-profit activity and having its seat within the country’s territory.

2. Exemptions under paragraph 1 do not apply to:
   - Alcoholic beverages;
   - Tobacco and tobacco products;
   - Transport vehicles intended for business activities;
   - Articles necessary for professional practice or occupation (excluding portable applied art items or exempted items) used by the deceased;
   - Stockpiles of raw materials, semi-finished products, or finished goods;
   - Livestock and agricultural product reserves exceeding household consumption needs.

3. Goods referred to in paragraph 1 qualify for exemption if declared for customs clearance procedures within 2 years from the final acquisition date of the inheritance by the entitled party. In exceptional cases, the competent head of the customs and tax office may extend this period.

— Tax exemption for inherited imports under Polish law applies to eligible individuals and entities, with exclusions for certain goods and conditions requiring timely customs declaration.
