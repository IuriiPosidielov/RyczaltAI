VAT Law – Remote Sales – Intermediary Appointment Rules

In cases referred to in Article 138b(1)(2), the taxpayer may appoint no more than one intermediary.

An intermediary may be an individual, legal entity, or organizational unit lacking legal personality, established by the taxpayer conducting remote sales of imported goods, and legally obligated to pay VAT and fulfill obligations under this chapter on behalf of the taxpayer, provided the following conditions are met:

1) Registered as an active VAT taxpayer;  
2) Has a registered business address or fixed place of business within the country chosen for submitting the declaration under Article 138e(3);  
3) For the past 24 months, has not been in arrears on individual taxes forming state budget revenue, exceeding separately 3% of the respective tax liabilities for each tax, where the arrears share is calculated relative to the amount due for the tax period in which the arrears arose;  
4) The individual (in the case of natural persons) or, for non-natural persons, the person who is a partner in a civil or commercial partnership lacking legal personality, a member of the management board, or the chief accountant, has not been convicted under the Tax Code of 1999 for a tax offense;  
5) Is authorized to provide professional tax advisory services under tax advisory regulations or to perform accounting services under accounting regulations.

The requirement under paragraph 3 is also deemed satisfied if the following conditions are jointly met:

1) The entity has settled, including interest for delay, tax arrears exceeding 3% of respective tax liabilities for each tax, within 30 days of their occurrence;  
2) Settlement of arrears under point 1 in the last 24 months has not concerned more than two separate tax periods for each tax.

The intermediary is appointed via a written agreement that includes:

1) Names and addresses of the parties, along with their VAT or equivalent tax identification data;  
2) The address where VAT-related documentation will be maintained;  
3) A statement by the appointed intermediary confirming compliance with the conditions in paragraph 2.

Keywords: VAT remote sales regulations define intermediary appointment limits, eligibility criteria for legal entities and individuals, and contractual requirements under EU VAT law.
