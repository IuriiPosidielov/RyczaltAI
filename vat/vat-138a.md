VAT Law – Definitions for Cross-Border E-Commerce and Import Procedures

1. VAT – refers to the value-added tax.
2. Distance sales of imported goods – refers to the sale of imported goods at a distance, excluding goods subject to excise duty, where the actual value of the parcel does not exceed the equivalent of 150 euros.
3. Non-resident taxpayer – refers to a taxpayer without a business establishment or fixed place of business in any EU Member State.
4. Member State of consumption – refers to the EU Member State where the delivery or transport of goods to the buyer concludes.
5. Member State of identification – refers to the EU Member State:
   a) chosen by a non-resident taxpayer to submit an information declaration regarding intent to use the import procedure;
   b) where the taxpayer has:
      – a business establishment, or
      – a fixed place of business (if no business establishment exists in the EU), or
      – a fixed place of business selected for submitting the import procedure declaration (if no EU business establishment exists but the taxpayer has multiple fixed places of business in the EU);
   c) where the intermediary has:
      – a business establishment, or
      – a fixed place of business (if no business establishment exists in the EU), or
      – a fixed place of business selected for submitting the import procedure declaration (if no EU business establishment exists but the intermediary has multiple fixed places of business in the EU).
6. Import procedure – refers to the VAT settlement due on the sale of imported goods at a distance, conducted via the Member State of identification for the Member State of consumption.

This article defines key terms for VAT obligations related to cross-border e-commerce and import procedures under EU VAT law, including non-resident taxpayers, delivery destination states, and identification states for VAT reporting purposes.
