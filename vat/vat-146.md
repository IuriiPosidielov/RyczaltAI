Polish VAT Law – Transitional Tax Rates (Art. 146) – Taxation of Goods and Services Prior to EU Accession

This article establishes transitional VAT rates applicable in Poland from the date of its accession to the EU until specified deadlines, defining tax rates for specific categories of goods and services, including exemptions and exclusions, particularly relating to construction, hospitality, and publications.

Key provisions:

1. 3% rate (until April 30, 2008)  
   Applies to services listed in Art. 5, concerning goods and services in Annex 6 to the Act, excluding intra-EU deliveries and exports.

2. 7% rate (as of December 31, 2007)  
   Applies to:
   - Construction, renovation, and maintenance works related to residential buildings and associated infrastructure;
   - Residential building objects or parts (excluding commercial units);
   - Food and beverage services, excluding:
     - Alcoholic beverages with >1.2% alcohol content and mixed alcoholic beverages (e.g., beer + non-alcoholic drinks with >0.5% alcohol);
     - Coffee, tea, carbonated non-alcoholic drinks, mineral water, and other goods taxed at 22% (in unprocessed state).

3. 0% rate (as of December 31, 2007)  
   Applies to:
   - Domestic and intra-EU acquisitions of:
     - Books (PKWiU ex 22.11) excluding address books, telephone directories, telegrams, fax, ISBN-marked music, maps, brochures;
     - Specialist periodicals;
   - Imports of:
     - ISBN-marked printed books and brochures (ex PCN 4901, CN 4901);
     - Specialist periodicals;
   - Conversion of rental rights into co-operative ownership rights in residential units, and transfer of ownership of residential units or single-family homes to co-operative members.

Definitions:

- Works related to residential construction and associated infrastructure — construction and renovation activities within residential buildings and associated infrastructure.
- Associated infrastructure includes:
  - Distribution networks with equipment, building connections;
  - Land development and planning for residential construction (roads, access, green spaces, small architecture);
  - Utility systems (water, sewage, heating, electricity, gas, telecommunications) connected to residential buildings.
- Specialist periodicals — periodic publications marked with ISSN, classified under PKWiU 22.13, PCN 4902, CN 4902, covering cultural, educational, scientific, social, professional, regional, local topics, intended for the blind and visually impaired, published no more than weekly, with a maximum print run of 15,000 copies, and not ending. Exclusions include:
  - General news publications covering national and international current events;
  - Publications with pornographic content, inciting hatred based on ethnicity, race, religion, or beliefs, or promoting totalitarian regimes;
  - Publications where at least 33% of space is dedicated to paid or unpaid advertisements;
  - Publications primarily containing complete novels, stories, or illustrated works;
  - Publications where over 20% of space is devoted to puzzles, crosswords, riddles, or games;
  - Popular publications with advice or profiles of public figures;
  - Publications without declared print runs.
