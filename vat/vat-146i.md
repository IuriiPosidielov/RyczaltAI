Tax Law – Polish VAT Regulation

The Minister of Finance, by means of regulation, may reduce VAT rates for certain goods and services, or for specific portions of such goods or services, and may establish conditions for applying reduced rates, to the following levels: 1) 0%, for cases and periods specified in Art. 146f; 2) 5%, for cases and periods specified in Art. 146f para. 1, point 3 or para. 2, point 3; 3) 6%, for cases and periods specified in Art. 146f para. 1, point 1 or para. 2, point 1; 4) 7%, for cases and periods specified in Art. 146f para. 1, point 2 or para. 2, point 2; 5) 8%, for cases and periods specified in Art. 146f para. 1, point 3 or para. 2, point 3; 6) 9%, for cases and periods specified in Art. 146f para. 1, point 1 or para. 2, point 1; 7) 10%, for cases and periods specified in Art. 146f para. 1, point 2 or para. 2, point 2 — taking into account the specific nature of trade in certain goods and services, the execution of the state budget, and EU regulations.

Polish VAT law permits the Minister of Finance to set reduced tax rates for specific goods and services under defined conditions, aligned with EU rules and budgetary considerations.
