**Tax Classification Request (WIS) – Legal Framework for Issuance and Submission Requirements**

This text outlines the legal provisions governing the issuance of a **WIS (Wniosek o Identyfikację Składu)** — a tax classification request — under Polish tax law, specifically Article 42b of the Tax Ordinance. It defines eligible applicants, required documentation, procedural obligations, and conditions under which the request may be denied.

**Eligible Applicants**:
- Taxpayer with a taxpayer identification number.
- Non-taxpayer entities performing or intending to perform activities specified in Article 42a.
- Public procurement contractors (under the Public Procurement Act of 2019) affecting pricing calculations.
- Public entities (under the Public-Private Partnership Act of 2008) affecting compensation calculations.
- Concessionaires (under the Concession Act of 2016) affecting compensation and payments.

**Submission Requirements**:
- Must be submitted via e-Urząd Skarbowy account with verified contact information (email or phone).
- Includes: applicant details, contact persons (if applicable), identification data, description of goods/services (with classification references to CN, Polish Classification of Goods/Services, or Construction Objects), and applicable legal provisions (for public entities/concessionaires).
- Must include declaration of no ongoing tax proceedings or unresolved cases at the time of submission — with criminal liability attached.

**Additional Provisions**:
- May request classification for non-tax-rate purposes (e.g., VAT exemptions, customs, public procurement).
- May cover single or multiple goods/services forming one taxable activity.
- May include supporting documents (photos, plans, certificates, manufacturer info) — mandatory upon request within 7 days.
- No WIS issued if subject is under active tax proceedings, resolution, or covered by investment agreements (Art. 20zt Ordynacji podatkowej).
- Denied requests trigger appeal rights.
