Taxation and Public Finance Law – Article on Place of Taxation Determination

The Minister responsible for public finance may, by regulation, designate alternative locations for taxation—other than those specified in Articles 22–26 and 28b–28n—when delivering goods, intra-community acquisition of goods, importing goods, or providing services, taking into account the specific nature of certain services, deliveries, intra-community acquisitions, or imports, as well as applicable EU regulations.

Public finance minister may regulate place of taxation for goods/services under EU rules, overriding default provisions in Articles 22–26 and 28b–28n, with consideration for service/goods-specific characteristics.
