Import Tax Exemption – Promotional Materials and Tourism-Related Documents

Art. 73. Import tax exemption applies to:

1) Promotional printed materials such as brochures, books, magazines, guides, unbound or bound posters, unbound photographs and photographic enlargements, illustrated or unillustrated geographic maps, and illustrated calendars whose primary purpose is to encourage international travel — particularly participation in cultural, tourism, sports, religious, or professional events — provided that:  
   a) they are intended for free distribution;  
   b) they contain no more than 25% private advertising surface area; and  
   c) they are promotional in nature.

2) Lists and directories of foreign hotels issued by official tourism institutions or under their patronage, and transportation schedules of transport services operating in third countries — provided that such documents contain no more than 25% private advertising surface area.

3) Materials sent to accredited representatives or correspondents of tourism institutions — not intended for distribution — including annual publications, subscriber telephone/telex lists, hotel directories, trade catalogues, samples of handicraft products of low value, documentation regarding museums, universities, health resorts, or similar institutions.

Import tax exemption for promotional and tourism-related printed materials and official documentation under Polish customs law.
