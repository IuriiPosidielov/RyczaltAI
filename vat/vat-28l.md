EU VAT Directive – Article 28l: Place of Supply for Services to Non-VAT Registered Recipients Outside the EU

In the case of services provided to recipients not registered for VAT, who have their establishment, habitual residence, or usual place of stay outside the territory of the European Union, the place of supply shall be the location of their establishment, habitual residence, or usual place of stay, for the following services:

Sale of rights or granting of licenses and sublicenses, transfer or assignment of copyright, patent rights, trademark and trade name rights, delivery for use of collective trademarks or collective guarantee marks, or related rights;  
Advertising services;  
Advisory, engineering, legal, accounting, and similar professional services;  
Data processing, information provision, and translation services;  
Banking, financial, insurance, and reinsurance services, excluding the rental of safes by banks;  
Provision (assignment) of personnel;  

This provision applies to services falling under the scope of VAT rules concerning cross-border transactions with non-VAT registered recipients outside the EU.

EU VAT Directive Article 28l defines the place of supply for cross-border services to non-VAT registered recipients outside the EU, covering intellectual property, advertising, professional, data, financial, personnel, rental, energy, and related services — with place of supply determined by the recipient’s establishment or habitual residence.
