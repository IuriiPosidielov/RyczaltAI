Tax Law – Customs and VAT Refund Procedures

The Minister of Finance shall, by regulation, determine the format of the mark referred to in Article 127(4)(3), the seal of the customs authority referred to in Article 128(3) and (5a), and detailed data to be included in the electronic TAX FREE document and its printed version.

When issuing the regulation under paragraph 1, the Minister shall ensure proper identification of goods, possibility of verifying export of goods by customs authorities, form in which tax refund shall be processed, accurate determination of the tax amount a traveler may claim, proper identification of seller and buyer, and correct indication at sales points of the TAX FREE mark to inform travelers that goods purchased there are eligible for tax refund.

The Minister may, by regulation, also specify additional goods (beyond those listed in Article 126(3)) for which Article 126(1) and (2) do not apply, minimum total value of purchases from the document under Article 128(2) required to qualify for a tax refund, and maximum commission rate under Article 128(6), taking into account the country’s economic situation, trade dynamics with third countries for specific product categories, market conditions for VAT-taxable goods, and EU regulations.
