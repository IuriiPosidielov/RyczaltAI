VAT Registration – Call-Off Stock Warehouse Notification (Poland)

A taxpayer or VAT taxpayer operating a warehouse into which goods are introduced under the call-off stock procedure must, within 14 days of the first introduction of goods into such warehouse, submit via electronic communication, in accordance with the electronic document format under the Act of February 17, 2005, on the informatization of activities of entities performing public tasks (Journal of Laws 2024, items 1557 and 1717), a notification concerning the operation of a warehouse used in the call-off stock procedure.

The notification must include:  
1) Place and purpose of submission;  
2) Taxpayer or VAT taxpayer details:  
   a) Name or full name;  
   b) Registered office or place of residence address;  
   c) Tax Identification Number (TIN) — for taxpayers;  
   d) VAT identification number assigned by the country where the taxpayer’s business seat or permanent establishment is located, and for individual VAT taxpayers — also date of birth;  
   e) VAT country-specific code corresponding to the issuing country’s VAT identification number;  
3) Warehouse address;  
4) Date of first goods introduction;  
5) Contact details of taxpayer, VAT taxpayer, or representative:  
   a) Full name;  
   b) Phone number.  

The notification may additionally include the taxpayer’s or VAT taxpayer’s email address or that of their representative.

If the notification fails to meet requirements under paragraphs 1 and 2, the head of the tax office shall, within 14 days of receipt, request its correction.

Any change to data in the notification must be reported within 14 days of the change via electronic means, following the same electronic document format.

Notifications under paragraphs 1 and 5 are submitted:  
1) By taxpayers to the head of the tax office;  
2) By VAT taxpayers to the head of the second tax office.
