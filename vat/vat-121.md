Legal Definition: Investment Gold – Polish Legislation (Article 121)

1. Where the term “investment gold” appears in the provisions of this chapter, it shall mean:
   - (1) Gold in the form of bars or ingots of at least 995 thousandths fineness, or gold represented by financial instruments;
   - (2) Gold coins meeting all of the following conditions:
     - a) Fineness of at least 900 thousandths;
     - b) Minted after the year 1800;
     - c) Were or are legal tender in the country of origin;
     - d) Sold at a price not exceeding 80% above the market value of the gold content.

2. Gold coins listed in the annual list published in Series C of the Official Journal of the European Union satisfy the conditions in paragraph 1, point 2, for the entire year of the list’s validity.

3. Gold coins referred to in paragraphs 1 and 2 are not considered numismatic collectibles.

This Polish legal provision defines “investment gold” for regulatory purposes, specifying eligible gold bars, ingots, and coins with minimum fineness, minting dates, legal tender status, and pricing caps — excluding numismatic value — under EU-aligned reporting in the Official Journal.
