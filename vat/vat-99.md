**VAT Declaration Filing Rules – Polish Tax Code (Art. 99)**

*Taxpayers subject to Art. 15 are required to file VAT returns monthly by the 25th of the following month, except as modified by Art. 130c, 133, 138g, and subsections 2–10 of Art. 99.*

*Small taxpayers using the cash method file quarterly returns by the 25th of the month following each quarter.*

*Small taxpayers not using the cash method, and taxpayers subject to the corporate income tax flat rate (Art. 28j of the Corporate Income Tax Act), may file quarterly returns by the 25th of the second month of the quarter — provided their annual turnover (including VAT) does not exceed €4 million, calculated using the average exchange rate of the Euro published by the NBP on the first working day of October of the previous tax year, rounded to the nearest 1,000 zł.*

*Exemptions from quarterly filing apply to: (1) VAT-registered active taxpayers for 12 months from registration; (2) taxpayers whose deliveries of goods listed in Annex 15 exceed €50,000/month over 4 preceding quarters; (3) importers under Art. 33a ust. 1; (4) taxpayers failing to provide payment instruments as required under Art. 19a of the Entrepreneurs Act (March 6, 2018) in places of actual business activity (including outside business premises or passenger transport vehicles).*

*Deliveries listed in Annex 15 are excluded if: (1) delivered at fuel stations or LPG stations into vehicle tanks; (2) delivered via the provider’s own transmission or distribution networks.*

*If turnover from Annex 15 deliveries exceeds the €4 million threshold, taxpayers must switch to monthly returns starting with the first month of the quarter in which the threshold was exceeded — filing the first return by the 25th of the month following the second month of the quarter if the threshold was breached in the second month; or by the 25th of the month following the third month if breached in the third month.*

*Importers under Art. 33a ust. 1 must switch to monthly returns starting with the first month of the quarter in which import occurred — filing the first return by the 25th of the month following the second month if the import occurred in the second month; or by the 25th of the month following the third month if in the third month.*

*Taxpayers failing to provide payment instruments as required under Art. 19a must switch to monthly returns starting with the first month of the quarter in which the violation was identified — filing the first return by the 25th of the month following the second month if identified in the second month; or by the 25th of the month following the third month if identified in the third month.*

*Small taxpayers whose turnover exceeds the threshold under Art. 2, point 25, must switch to monthly returns starting with the first month of the quarter in which the threshold was exceeded — filing the first return by the 25th of the month following the second month if the threshold was breached in the second month; or by the 25th of the month following the third month if breached in the third month.*

*These rules apply to taxpayers who: (1) have voluntarily abandoned the cash method; (2) were previously eligible for the corporate income tax flat rate (Art. 28j) but no longer meet the conditions.*

*Art. 99, Art. 1 does not apply to taxpayers whose sales are exempt under Art. 113 or 113a, or entities performing only exempt activities under Art. 43 ust. 1 or Art. 82 ust. 3 — unless they are entitled to a VAT refund under Art. 87 or Art. 92 ust. 1 pkt 3, or are required to adjust previously deducted VAT under Art. 90a, 90c, 91, or required to adjust under Art. 89a ust. 4 or 89b ust. 1, or are entitled to adjust under Art. 89b ust. 4.*

*If a taxpayer suspends business activity under applicable suspension rules, they are not required to file returns for periods covered by the suspension — except for: (1) intra-community acquisitions; (2) imports of services or goods; (3) periods where suspension did not cover the entire reporting period; (4) periods requiring VAT adjustment or tax reconciliation.*

*VAT returns (Art. 1–3, 3c–6, 8–9, 11a) must be submitted electronically, using a standardized electronic document format (Art. 109 ust. 3 of the 2005 IT Act for public sector entities), transmitted via procedures set by Art. 193a § 3 of the Tax Ordinance.*

*VAT returns (Art. 8–9, 10, 11, 11a) must be filed electronically using standardized formats, with instructions and deadlines defined by the Minister of Finance via regulation — to enable correct VAT reconciliation and control by tax authorities.*

*Returns for taxpayers who have appointed a VAT representative (Art. 18d ust. 1) must be filed by the representative on behalf of the taxpayer by the 25th of the month following each month.*

*Taxpayers who only make intra-community acquisitions of new transport equipment must file a return within 14 days of the tax liability arising.*

*Taxpayers under Art. 16 file returns by the last day of the month following the month in which the tax liability arises.*

*For intra-community acquisitions under Art. 103 ust. 5a, VAT returns must be filed by the 5th of the month following the month in which the tax liability arises — the payer files the return if applicable under Art. 17a.*

*The Minister of Finance shall: (1) publish the electronic document template for returns under Art. 11c on the e-government platform; (2) define detailed data requirements, filing instructions, and submission locations under Art. 7c via regulation — ensuring proper VAT reconciliation and tax authority control; (3) define return formats and instructions under Arts. 8–14c via regulation — enabling proper VAT reconciliation and control.*

*All VAT returns must be submitted electronically using standardized formats and procedures defined by the Minister of Finance.*

*Tax returns must include all data necessary for VAT calculation, including taxable base, tax due, tax paid, and taxpayer information — in accordance with Art. 7c.*

*Returns under Arts. 1–3, 3c–6, 8–9, and 11a must be submitted via electronic means only.*

*Taxpayers are not required to file returns under Arts. 1–3 or 8 if they are exempt under Art. 17 ust. 1 pkt 4 and 5 — in which case, returns are due by the 25th of the month following the month in which the tax liability arises.*

*Taxpayers under Art. 15 who do not have an obligation to file under Arts. 1–3 or 8, but who make intra-community acquisitions, must file returns for those acquisitions by the 25th of the month following the month in which the tax liability arises.*

*Returns under Art. 8a must be filed by the VAT representative on behalf of the taxpayer by the 25th of the month following each month — for intra-community supplies.*

*Returns under Arts. 10 and 11 must be filed by the taxpayer within 14 days of the tax liability arising — for intra-community acquisitions or new transport equipment.*

*Returns under Art. 11a must be filed by the payer — for intra-community acquisitions under Art. 103 ust. 5a — by the 5th of the month following the month in which the tax liability arises.*
