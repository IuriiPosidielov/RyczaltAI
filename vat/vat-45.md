**Customs Import Exemptions – Polish Tax Code Article 45**

1. Exemption from import duty applies to:
   - Goods undergoing active refining procedures;
   - Goods subject to temporary admission with full exemption from import duties;
   - Gold imported by the National Bank of Poland;
   - Goods re-imported from third-country territory, previously exported by the taxpayer and exempt from customs duties;
   - Fish and seafood products (unprocessed or preserved for delivery) imported by entities engaged in marine fishing;
   - Human organs and human breast milk;
   - Human blood, plasma, blood cells, or blood-derived preparations (non-pharmaceutical);
   - Currency, banknotes, and coins used as legal tender (excluding numismatic items: gold/silver coins or non-standard banknotes);
   - Military supplies for NATO forces (excluding Poland) for their own use or personnel support during joint defense operations;
   - Military supplies for EU member states’ forces (excluding Poland) for their own use or personnel support during EU security and defense actions;
   - Natural gas entering or being introduced into the gas system, or energy (electricity, heat, cooling) delivered via distribution networks;
   - Low-value shipments (≤150 EUR equivalent) excluding excisable goods, provided:
     a) VAT on import is declared under special procedures (Chapter XII, Section 9);
     b) Valid VAT identification number is provided at import (per Art. 138e(7)(1)–(2)).

2. Exemptions under para. 1, pts. 2 and 5 do not apply if customs duties are otherwise due.

3. Exemption under para. 1, pt. 1 does not apply if:
   a) Customs duties are otherwise due;
   b) Active refining procedure has been formally closed under Art. 324 of Regulation 2015/2447.

—  
*Customs import exemptions in Poland under Article 45 cover specific goods, services, and entities, with conditions tied to procedural compliance and duty liability.*
