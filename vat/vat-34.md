**Customs Law – Import Tax Determination and Appeal Procedures**

Article 34.  
1. In cases not covered by Articles 33(1)–(3), the head of the customs and tax office determines the amount of import duty by decision.  
1a. If the decision under paragraph 1 fails to specify the tax amount in the correct amount, the head of the customs and tax office issues a supplementary decision stating the difference between the correct amount and the previously determined amount.  
2. For tax liabilities established under Article 33(1), except those specified in Article 33a, the relevant customs notification rules regarding the debtor apply.  
3. Parties may appeal decisions under paragraphs 1 and 1a, and Article 33(2)–(3), to the Director of the Tax Administration Chamber, via the head of the customs and tax office that issued the decision.  
4. Proceedings under paragraphs 1, 1a, and 3, and Article 33(2)–(3) are governed by:  
   (1) Articles 5, 18, 19, 22(1) and (2), (2) and (3), (4)(1), (6) and (7), 23(1)–(3), 27, 28(1)–(3) and (4)(1) of the EU Customs Code, and regulations issued under Articles 20, 21, 24, 25, 31, and 32 of the EU Customs Code;  
   (2) Articles 10a, 10b, 73c, 76, 77, 83, 84, 86, 88–90, and 90b–90e of the Customs Act of March 19, 2004;  
   (3) The Tax Ordinance, except that in Part IV of the Act, Articles 138a(4), 141–143, 168, 170, 215(1), and Chapters 2, 5, 6, 9, and 10, Chapter 11 (excluding Article 200), Chapters 16a and 21–23 apply; appeals also follow Articles 140(1), 162(1)–(3), 163(2), 169, 208, 210(1) paragraphs 1–6 and 8 and (2), 220, 221, 222, 223, 226–229, 232, 233(1) and (2), 234, and 234a of the Act.  
5. Actions taken under paragraphs 1, 1a, and 3, and Article 33(2)–(3), based on the Tax Ordinance — except decisions under Articles 163(2), 169(4), 179(2), 215(1), 228(1), 262(5), 263(1), 268(3), and 270a —:  
   (1) are not considered decisions under customs law;  
   (2) are only appealable via appeals against decisions.
