VAT Directive – Definitions and Key Terms (EU Tax Law)

1) VAT – refers to the Value Added Tax and the tax on added value.  
1a) Services – refers to services provided to persons who are not VAT taxpayers as defined in Article 28a, where the place of provision is within the territory of the consumer Member State.  
2) Non-resident entities – refers to legal persons, organizational units without legal personality, and natural persons without a business establishment or fixed place of business within the European Union, carrying out VAT-taxable activities on EU territory.  
3a) Consumer Member State – refers to the Member State where the service is provided.  
4) Identifying Member State – refers to the Member State chosen by a non-resident entity to submit a notification regarding its intention to use the non-EU procedure, if its service provision as a VAT taxpayer within the EU commences under the provisions of this chapter.  
6) Non-EU procedure – refers to the VAT settlement process for services rendered to the consumer Member State, carried out through the identifying Member State.

Keywords: VAT definitions for cross-border service provision under EU VAT rules, including roles of consumer and identifying Member States, and the non-EU procedure for non-resident entities.
