VAT Law – Intracommunity Goods Acquisition Rules

Art. 26. 1. In the case where the buyer in an intra-community acquisition of goods is:  
1) a taxpayer – Article 25(2)(2) applies only if the taxpayer provides, in the summary information referred to in Article 100(1), details of transactions conducted by them in the context of a three-party transaction, relating to intra-community acquisitions and deliveries of goods;  
2) a taxpayer or a legal entity not a taxpayer who has not provided, in accordance with Article 97(10), a tax identification number prefixed with “PL” – the intra-community acquisition of goods shall be deemed to have taken place within the country’s territory, subject to paragraph 2.  

2. The provision of paragraph 1(2) does not apply if the buyer, for intra-community acquisition of goods, has provided a number assigned to them for such transactions on the territory of another EU Member State, provided that the place of completion of delivery or transport of goods is not located within the country’s territory.
