VAT Act of March 11, 2004 Article 106e, Paragraph 1 – Invoice Requirements and Taxation Details
This document outlines the mandatory elements that must appear on an invoice under applicable tax regulations. Each component is essential for proper identification, tax calculation, and compliance.
Article 106e, Paragraph 1: The invoice must include the following information:
1. Date of issue — The exact date when the invoice was created.
2. Consecutive invoice number — A unique identifier assigned within one or more series, ensuring each invoice can be distinctly identified.
3. Taxpayer and purchaser details — Full names and surnames (or names) of both the seller and buyer, along with their complete addresses.
4. Taxpayer identification number — The number used to identify the taxpayer for tax purposes, as defined in point 24(a) and point 25 of the regulations.
5. Purchaser identification number — The number used to identify the purchaser for tax or VAT purposes, subject to point 24(b), points 26, and 27. This provision will come into force on 1 February 2026 (Journal of Laws of 2025).
6. Date of delivery or service provision — The date when goods were delivered or services were performed, as specified in Article 106b, paragraph 1, point 4, or item 1203. If payment was received on a specific date different from the invoice date, that date must be indicated.
7. Description of goods or services — The name or type of the product or service provided.
8. Quantity or scope — The measure or number of goods delivered, or the scope of services performed.
9. Unit price (net) — The price per unit without any tax amount included.
10. Discounts or reductions — Any discounts or price reductions, including rebates for advance payments, unless already included in the unit price net.
11. Net sales value — The total value of goods delivered or services performed, excluding tax.
12. Tax rate — The applicable VAT rate or special tax rate, as defined in Sections XII, chapters 6a, 7, and 9.
13. Net sales value breakdown — The net sales value divided according to applicable tax rates and tax-exempt portions.
14. Tax amount breakdown — The tax calculated on the net sales value, broken down by individual tax rates.
15. Total amount due — The sum of the net sales value plus tax.
16. Cash method indication — When tax liability arises based on Article 19a, paragraph 5, point 1, or Article 21, paragraph 1, or when regulations under Article 19a, paragraph 12 specify that tax liability arises upon receipt of payment (partial or full), the term “cash method” must be included.
17. Self-billing indication — For invoices issued under Article 106d, paragraph 1, the term “self-billing” must be included.
18. Reverse charge indication — when the purchaser of goods or services is responsible for settling VAT or a similar tax, the term "reverse charge" must be included.
18a. Split payment mechanism — When the total amount due exceeds 15,000 PLN (or its equivalent in foreign currency), including transactions with taxpayers listed in Annex No. 15, the term “split payment mechanism” must be included. For conversion of foreign currency amounts into PLN, the official exchange rates used for tax base determination must be applied.
19. Exempt transaction indication — For transactions involving delivery of goods or provision of services that are exempt from tax, the appropriate exemption notation must be included.
