Customs Duty Exemption for Agricultural and Related Products from Third-Country Farms Adjacent to Poland’s Border

Art. 54. 1. Exemption from import duties applies to agricultural products, livestock products, beekeeping, horticulture, and forestry products obtained from farms located in third countries bordering Poland, operated by Polish farmers who maintain their primary agricultural enterprise within Poland — provided all of the following conditions are met:  
1) For livestock products — animals must have been bred within Poland or permitted for trade within Poland;  
2) Products must have undergone only processing typically performed after harvest or production;  
3) Customs declaration for market authorization must be submitted by the farmer or on their behalf.

2. The provisions of paragraph 1 apply accordingly to fishery products and inland water fisheries conducted by Polish fishermen along Poland’s border waters and lakes, and hunting products obtained by Polish hunters in those same waters and lakes.

— Exemption from import duties applies to specific agricultural and related products from adjacent third-country farms, subject to conditions including origin of livestock, limited post-harvest processing, and customs declaration by the Polish farmer or their representative.
