Tax Law – VAT Directive Implementation – Article 103b – Tax Liability Allocation in Cross-Border Supply Situations

In cases where, under Article 5c of Regulation 282/2011, a VAT-registered supplier facilitating the delivery of goods referred to in Article 7a(1) and (2) is not obligated to pay VAT exceeding the amount they have declared and paid, the head of the tax office or the head of the customs and tax office shall determine the amount of VAT payable to the recipient of the goods acting on behalf of that supplier.

The amount of VAT to be paid, as referred to in paragraph 1, is the amount for which, under Article 5c of Regulation 282/2011, the VAT-registered supplier facilitating the delivery of goods is not required to pay.

Summary: VAT liability allocation under EU VAT Directive 282/2011 for goods supplied via facilitation by a VAT-registered entity, governed by national tax authority determination.
