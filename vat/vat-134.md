International Transport Services – Non-EU Procedure – Electronic Recordkeeping Obligations

Foreign entities identified for the purpose of non-EU procedures are required to maintain electronic records of transactions subject to such procedures in accordance with the requirements set out in Article 63c of Regulation 282/2011. These records must be made available electronically upon request by the identifying Member State and the consuming Member State. In the event of technical difficulties preventing electronic access, records shall be provided in the manner and timeframe specified by the competent tax authority. Records must be retained for 10 years from the end of the tax year in which the international transport service was performed.

Foreign entities subject to non-EU VAT procedures must maintain and provide electronic transaction records for 10 years, accessible to relevant Member States, with fallback provisions for technical failures.
