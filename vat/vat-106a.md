VAT Scope of Application – Article 106a (Polish VAT Law)

This article defines the scope of application of the provisions of this chapter, which governs VAT obligations in cross-border transactions and specific VAT procedures. It applies to:

1) Sales, excluding:
   a) Transactions covered by Article 17(1)(4) and (5) and (1a), where the service provider or goods supplier does not charge VAT and the invoice is not issued by the recipient or buyer on behalf of the supplier or provider;
   b) Transactions subject to special procedures under Chapter XII, Sections 6a, 7, and 9, where the identifying Member State is not the Republic of Poland.

2) Supply of goods and services by VAT taxpayers with a business establishment or fixed place of business in the country, from which the supply is performed; or, in the absence of such, taxpayers with a permanent residence or habitual place of stay in the country, from which the supply is performed, where the place of supply is:
   a) In a Member State other than the country, and the VAT liability rests with the buyer or service recipient, and the invoice is not issued by the buyer or recipient on behalf of the supplier;
   b) In a third country.

3) Supply of goods and services subject to special procedures under Chapter XII, Sections 6a and 7, performed by taxpayers identified under those procedures, where the identifying Member State under the provisions of those sections is the Republic of Poland.

4) Distance sales of imported goods, processed under the special procedure in Chapter XII, Section 9, performed by taxpayers identified under that procedure, where the identifying Member State under the provisions of this section is the Republic of Poland.
