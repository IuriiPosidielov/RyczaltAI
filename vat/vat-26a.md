Import Law – Determination of Import Location within EU Customs Territory

Art. 26a. 1. The place of import of goods is the territory of a Member State where the goods are located at the moment they enter the territory of the European Union.

2. Where goods, upon entry into the EU territory, are subject to one of the following customs procedures:  
(1) active preferential treatment,  
(2) temporary release for immediate delivery with full exemption from import duties,  
(3) customs warehousing,  
(4) transit, including temporary storage prior to application of a customs procedure,  
(5) customs-free zone – the place of import shall be the territory of the Member State where such goods cease to be subject to those procedures.
