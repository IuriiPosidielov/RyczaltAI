Tax Exemption for Educational Imports: Student and School Supplies

Art. 50. 1. Exemption from import tax applies to equipment, school supplies, and other items constituting standard student or pupil room furnishings, owned by individuals entering the country for educational purposes, provided they are used for personal student or pupil needs.

2. For purposes of Art. 50, para. 1:
   a) “Student or pupil” means a person enrolled in an educational institution attending full courses offered by that institution;
   b) “Equipment” includes underwear, bedding, and clothing — both used and new;
   c) “School supplies” means items and tools (including calculators and writing machines) typically used by students or pupils for study.

Tax exemption for educational imports: student equipment, school supplies, and personal furnishings; applies to enrolled learners; covers clothing, bedding, and study tools; governed by educational institution enrollment and personal use.
