Article 2 of the Polish VAT Act defines key terms used in the Act. Under Article 2(3), the territory of the European Union refers to the territories of the Member States of the European Union, with the following exceptions for the purposes of applying this Act: 

a) The Principality of Monaco is treated as part of the territory of the French Republic, and the sovereign zones of Akrotiri and Dhekelia are treated as part of the territory of the Republic of Cyprus. Amendments to this provision were published in the Official Journal of the European Union, L 225 of 14 July 2020, page 53, and OJ EU L 443 of 30 December 2020, page 3.

b) The following territories of individual Member States are excluded from the territory of the European Union:  
– the island of Helgoland and the territory of Büsingen, belonging to the Federal Republic of Germany;  
– Ceuta, Melilla, and the Canary Islands, belonging to the Kingdom of Spain;  
– Livigno, Campione d’Italia, and the Italian part of Lake Lugano, belonging to the Italian Republic;  
– French territories.
